Dataset Overview

This dataset includes physicochemical properties of red and white wines such as acidity, alcohol, sulphates, and pH. The goal is to predict wine quality on a scale of 0 to 10.

Source context: Portuguese Vinho Verde wines with ~11-12 input features plus quality score.

Dataset Features

Alcohol

Higher alcohol often correlates with stronger body and potentially higher quality scores.

Acidity

Volatile acidity, when too high, can create undesirable aromas and lower perceived quality. Balanced acidity supports freshness and structure.

Sulphates

Sulphates can improve microbial stability and influence mouthfeel. Moderate levels are usually associated with better preservation and balance.

pH

pH indicates acidity strength and influences color stability, flavor balance, and microbial risk. Lower pH generally means brighter acidity and more stability.
